Goat & cheese

The Creamy

The Drunken Goat

Murcia, Spain

The Cabra al Vino (goat with wine) is a semi-soft, smoked cheese. This truly creamy, sweet and smooth cheese hails from the Spanish Mediterraean coast. It has a lovely rind, stained purple from a 48-72 hour curing in red wine.

The Sharp

Orange Windmill Gouda Goat Cablanca

The Netherlands

This creamy cheese is mild and sweet but packs a citrus tang. It's firmer than your typical goat cheese and makes for a great gouda to serve with jams.

The Smokey

Cypress Grove Humbolt Fog Grande

California

This cheese is not smokey in the typical sense, like a smoked cheddar. It instead derives it's earthy, smokiness from its edible ash rind. It's buttery and creamy and is one of the earliest soft-ripened goat cheeses made in America.

The Earthy

Vermont Creamery Herb Chevré

Vermont

Herbs du Provence is one of my favorite spice combinations to cook with, and this soft, creamy cheese is rolled in it. Spread this herbal, lavender mix on a hot slice of baguette and be transported to Provencial France immediately.
